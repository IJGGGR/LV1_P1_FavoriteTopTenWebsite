10. Five Nights at Freddy's: Sister Location

This main series entry made a huge departure from the established gameplay loops of previous entries, instead opting to have a story-focused progression with a bunch of tedious mini-games sprinkled in. It's only saving grace is the two office gameplay sections, that aren't available regularly. One is found via the secret ending route and the other is the custom night.

The embedded video is a thorough criticism of Five Nights at Freddy's: Sister Location.

9. Five Nights at Freddy's: Security Breach

This main series entry made a huge departure from the established gameplay loops of previous entries, instead creating a story-focused and open-world progression. Its launch was riddled with bugs and crashes that were very slowly fixed, but not everything was fixed. The game is best experienced by playing it as a comedy game, rather than a horror game.

The embedded video is a positive review of Five Nights at Freddy's: Security Breach.

8. Five Nights at Freddy's 2

This main series entry continued the gameplay loop established in the previous game. It did however introduce too many threats to deal with and became generally overwhelming. It also made the camera system mostly useless.

The embedded video is a positive review of Five Nights at Freddy's 2.

7. Five Nights at Freddy's: Security Breach - Ruin

This expansion addressed many of the gripes fans had with the base game. It added a story-focused but linear progression. It was also much better at being a horror game.

The embedded video is a positive review of Five Nights at Freddy's: Security Breach - Ruin.

6. Five Nights at Freddy's 3

This main series entry continued the gameplay loop established in the previous games. This time instead focusing on one deadly threat, and many detrimental threats. It also made the camera system a fundamental aspect of the gameplay.

The embedded video is a positive review of Five Nights at Freddy's 3.

5. Ultimate Custom Night

This non-main series entry was a long awaited encapsulation of everything fans wanted for an "ultimate custom night". It came with many mini challenges players could do to learn the mechanics of each threat while also being interesting in the process of building up to the max difficulty.

The embedded video is a review of Ultimate Custom Night.

4. Five Nights at Freddy's: Secret of the Mimic

This main series entry made a continued departure from the established gameplay loops of previous entries, continuing the story-focused exploration with linear progression. It was completely filled with lore that fans were starving for.

The embedded video is a collaborative discussion of Five Nights at Freddy's: Secret of the Mimic.

3. Freddy Fazbear's Pizzeria Simulator

This non-main series entry made a slight departure from the gameplay loop established in the previous games. This time creating a hybrid of tycoon elements and office gameplay. It also incorporated many mini-games that further expanded the lore in ways fans loved. This game also contains the single best moment of the entire franchise, the "Connection Terminated" cutscene that is found in the Completion Ending.

The embedded video is a positive review of Freddy Fazbear's Pizzeria Simulator.

2. Five Nights at Freddy's 4

This main series entry made a slight departure from the gameplay loop established in the previous games, this time completely changing the office gameplay by removing the camera system and instead focusing entirely on door defense. The atmosphere and ambience creates a perfect horror for tense gameplay.

The embedded video is a positive review of Five Nights at Freddy's 4.

1. Five Nights at Freddy's

The original game, originally made out of spite due to his prior games not being particularly well-received. This game was a nearly perfect first try at horror. The atmosphere and ambience mixed with the frantic camera switching most players did out of panic trying to avoid the animatronic jumpscares kickstarted one of the most successful franchises that has since gone on to release more video games, books, and movies.
